# Proposal Routes

Choose one primary route. A proposal may borrow page types from other routes, but the primary route determines the storyline.

## Universal Persuasion Spine

Use this spine unless a tender format requires a fixed order:

1. Cover and winning thesis
2. Client problem / brief translation
3. Decision standards
4. Evidence and insight
5. Core strategic judgment
6. Solution system
7. Module execution
8. Proof, samples, and cases
9. Cadence, team, KPI, budget, risk
10. Closing recommendation and next steps

## Route A: Brand, Social, and Content Marketing

Use for brand marketing, social operation, creative campaigns, content systems, young-audience strategy, creator plans, and platform communication.

Client decision usually asks: Do you understand the audience and platform language? Can you create recognizable content? Can the idea become a repeatable operating system?

Recommended chapters:

1. Brand challenge and communication opportunity
2. User/platform/competitor observations
3. Core content or brand-personality thesis
4. Platform roles and account architecture
5. Content pillars, columns, samples, and adaptation
6. Operating cadence and growth loop
7. Brand safety and review rules
8. Team, KPI, budget, and cases

Required proof objects:

- platform screenshot analysis
- content pillar map
- sample scripts/storyboards/mock posts
- platform role matrix
- monthly cadence
- brand boundary / safety matrix

## Route B: Annual Operation and Retainer Service

Use for annual operation plans, account operation, B2B content assets, service retainers, always-on social, CRM/lead operation, and customer-management systems.

Client decision usually asks: Can this team run the work every month, keep quality stable, and turn content into assets?

Recommended chapters:

1. Scope and brief response
2. Current asset / historical data read
3. Growth gap and operating thesis
4. Account, platform, and asset system
5. Monthly/quarterly operating mechanism
6. Team collaboration and review process
7. KPI and measurement layers
8. Base service budget, exclusions, and optional modules
9. Cases, assurance, and next-step recommendation

Required proof objects:

- brief-to-delivery map
- current asset audit
- operating loop
- monthly work calendar
- responsibility matrix
- budget boundary table
- KPI tree

## Route C: Project Execution, Live, E-Commerce, and Activation

Use for live commerce, event execution, campaign activation, production-heavy work, channel launches, complex budgets, and projects where delivery certainty matters as much as strategy.

Client decision usually asks: Can you execute without losing control of schedule, budget, quality, and risk?

Recommended chapters:

1. Project objective and success standard
2. Scenario / audience / conversion logic
3. Creative or experience concept
4. Execution model and production system
5. Run-of-show, SOP, resource plan, and asset reuse
6. Traffic, conversion, data, and review mechanism
7. Budget by cost category and acceptance standard
8. Risk register, contingency plan, and evidence pack
9. Team, credentials, cases, and closing

Required proof objects:

- ROS / timeline
- production workflow
- role and responsibility table
- asset reuse matrix
- process KPI dashboard
- budget category table
- risk register
- evidence-pack checklist

## Route D: Consulting, Digital, AI, SaaS, and Tooling

Use for consulting plans, digital transformation, AI tools, SaaS/platform proposals, data products, workflow automation, and technical service proposals.

Client decision usually asks: Is the diagnosis credible? Is the roadmap realistic? Can the system integrate with existing operations?

Recommended chapters:

1. Business problem and transformation target
2. Current-state diagnosis
3. Opportunity and value hypothesis
4. Solution architecture
5. Use cases and workflow changes
6. Implementation roadmap
7. Data, security, governance, and adoption plan
8. KPI and value measurement
9. Commercial model, team, risk, and next steps

Required proof objects:

- current-state map
- opportunity matrix
- architecture diagram
- use-case cards
- phased roadmap
- governance matrix
- value/KPI model

## Route E: Partnership, Sponsorship,招商, and Resource Integration

Use for sponsorship,招商, channel cooperation, joint marketing, venue/product partnerships, resource packages, and strategic cooperation.

Client decision usually asks: Why is this opportunity valuable, credible, and easy to activate?

Recommended chapters:

1. Partnership opportunity
2. Audience / market / resource fit
3. Cooperation proposition
4. Rights and resource package
5. Activation scenarios
6. Media/content/traffic plan
7. Investment package and return logic
8. Implementation timeline, roles, and risk
9. Closing invitation and next step

Required proof objects:

- audience-resource fit matrix
- rights package table
- activation scenario map
- media plan
- investment tier table
- partner responsibility matrix
