# Page Types and Proof Objects

Use this as a page-planning library. Every proposal slide needs a page purpose and a proof object.

## Required Planning Fields

For each slide, plan:

- `slide_no`
- `title`: conclusion sentence
- `page_purpose`: what the client should decide or believe
- `key_message`: one sentence
- `proof_object`: visible evidence or structured object
- `visual_form`: layout choice
- `source_status`: confirmed / needs source /待确认
- `script_intent`: what the presenter explains orally

## Core Page Types

### Cover

Purpose: frame the proposal in one sentence.

Proof object: project name, client name, period, presenter/company, one-line thesis.

Avoid: crowded credentials, multiple slogans, generic hero copy.

### Brief Translation

Purpose: prove the team understood the real purchase criteria.

Proof object: table mapping `client requirement -> our interpretation -> proposal response -> evidence/slide`.

Use early. Do not merely restate the brief.

### Decision Standards

Purpose: define how the client should judge the proposal.

Proof object: 3-5 criteria with why each matters.

Examples: business fit, platform judgment, execution certainty, budget clarity, measurable outcomes, risk control.

### Insight / Evidence Read

Purpose: establish the facts that justify the strategy.

Proof object: data chart, screenshot cluster, competitor comparison, user task map, category observation.

Rule: every insight must lead to an action later in the deck.

### Transferable Benchmark

Purpose: learn from competitors or benchmark cases without copying them.

Proof object: `what they do -> why it works -> what can transfer -> what not to copy`.

Avoid: screenshot gallery with no method extraction.

### Core Strategic Judgment

Purpose: state the main strategic choice.

Proof object: thesis card, 3-pillar model, before/after shift, decision matrix.

Use no more than 1-3 slides for core judgment.

### Strategy Map

Purpose: show the whole solution at a glance.

Proof object: system diagram with 3-5 pillars and relationship lines.

Must make chapter sequence easier to follow.

### Platform / Channel Role Matrix

Purpose: explain why each platform exists in the plan.

Proof object: matrix of `platform -> role -> content/action -> KPI -> assets`.

Avoid: equal treatment of every platform when the budget or audience does not support it.

### Content / Product Mechanism

Purpose: prove the idea can become repeatable output.

Proof object: content pillars, column system, sample episode, storyboard, SKU/scenario mapping, asset reuse matrix.

Include samples when the proposal sells creative judgment.

### Execution Cadence

Purpose: turn strategy into operating rhythm.

Proof object: 30-day cold start, 12-week roadmap, monthly cadence, quarterly rhythm, annual calendar.

Show progression, not only dates.

### SOP / Workflow

Purpose: prove delivery control.

Proof object: steps, owners, timing, inputs, outputs, review points.

Keep to 3-6 major steps unless using an appendix.

### Team and Collaboration

Purpose: explain who does what and how quickly the team responds.

Proof object: role table, RACI, communication rhythm, escalation path.

Avoid: resume dumping. Show operating roles first.

### KPI and Measurement

Purpose: make success measurable without overpromising.

Proof object: KPI tree by layer: exposure, engagement, search/asset, conversion, retention, brand asset, business handoff.

Mark baselines and targets as待确认 if not provided.

### Budget / Commercial Terms

Purpose: make money explainable and defensible.

Proof object: `amount -> purpose -> deliverable -> frequency -> acceptance -> exclusions`.

Split base service, project modules, optional items, media/third-party costs, client-provided resources, and assumptions.

### Case / Credential Evidence

Purpose: prove capability and relevance.

Proof object: case card with `context -> action -> transferable method -> evidence`.

Use only verified cases. If results are private or unavailable, say so.

### Risk Control

Purpose: reduce client fear.

Proof object: `risk -> trigger -> response -> owner -> backup`.

Common risks: brand safety, public opinion, review delay, budget variance, copyright, platform policy, production schedule, talent availability, data attribution.

### Brief Coverage Check

Purpose: prove every important requirement is answered.

Proof object: checklist with slide references and status.

Useful before closing in formal tenders.

### Closing Recommendation

Purpose: return to the winning thesis and make the next action clear.

Proof object: 3 reasons to choose this plan, recommended package, next-step timeline.

Avoid: generic `THANKS` as the only closing message.
