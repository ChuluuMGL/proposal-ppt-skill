# editorial-brand Style Spec

## Position

Editorial, image-led, premium consumer tone. Use when taste, cultural fluency, brand feeling, or product desire is part of the buying decision.

## Color System

| Role | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Paper | `#F3ECE0` | Warm editorial canvas |
| Secondary paper | `#ECE2D2` / `#E4D8C4` | Caption blocks, page divisions |
| Ink | `#1C1814` | Main serif headlines and body |
| Soft ink | `#3A342C` | Body copy and captions |
| Muted | `#8C8170` | Folios, source notes, metadata |
| Rule | `#D3C6B0` | Thin editorial dividers |
| Green | `#2F4A37` | Brand / cultural emphasis |
| Clay | `#9A5A38` | Warm accent, quote line, business implication |
| Seal red | `#B0382C` | Rare proof or warning accent |

Rules:

- Warm paper plus high-contrast type is the core, not beige card grids.
- Use clay/green as editorial emphasis; do not color every label.
- Red is used as a seal or warning, never as broad decoration.

## Typography

| Layer | Font | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Main headings | Songti SC / Source Han Serif SC / Noto Serif SC | Editorial serif, high contrast, large scale |
| English accent | Playfair Display / Cormorant Garamond | Kicker, folio, quote marks, category labels |
| Body | PingFang SC / system sans or readable serif | Keep captions concise and proof-oriented |
| Numbers | Playfair Display / Georgia | Large year, budget total, issue numbers |

Rules:

- The serif headline must carry a real claim, not a decorative slogan.
- Captions must explain business relevance: what the image proves and how it transfers.

## Image And Asset Behavior

- Prefer product macro, lifestyle scene, campaign still, creator/sample image, cultural reference, or art-directed AI concept image.
- Crop images like magazine spreads: one dominant image or a deliberate proof board, not random equal cards.
- Every image needs a caption rail or adjacent explanation.
- Do not use generic stock atmosphere, blurry hands, or low-resolution screenshots.

## Charts And Tables

- Keep business pages clear: thin-rule tables on paper background.
- Budget/KPI pages use editorial typography but business-clean structure.
- Charts should use direct labels, minimal grid, and one editorial accent.
- Avoid glossy infographic decoration that weakens proof.

## Module System

| Module | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Cover | Large editorial thesis + year/scope rail |
| Insight | Pull quote + cultural or product proof |
| Strategy | Three editorial pillars with captioned implication |
| Content sample | Moodboard, campaign sample, or image proof board |
| Budget | Thin-rule ledger + boundary sidebar |
| Close | Editorial thesis returning to the brand promise |

## Reject Rules

- Cream background plus serif title with no image/proof logic.
- Repeated equal cards pretending to be editorial.
- Image without business caption.
- Budget/KPI page becomes art board and loses readability.
- Real or fake client brand names in public demos.
